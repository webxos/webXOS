The Concept

This framework applies the Circle of Fifths from music theory to organize LLM latent spaces into 12 semantic hubs (keys). Each key represents a different "tonality" or style in language generation.

  • Circle Structure: Maps the latent space as a 2D/3D manifold with 12 core concepts
  • Fifths Relationship: Uses information geometric distance to measure semantic similarity
  • Relative Minors: Inner ring represents implicit, emotional contexts (latent styles)
  • Harmonic Modulation: Moving around the circle changes output style/tonality

Interactive Concept Explanation

The Circle of Fifths

In music theory, the Circle of Fifths organizes keys by their relationship to each other. Moving clockwise adds sharps (increasing tension), while counter-clockwise adds flats (decreasing tension).

Mapping to LLM Latent Space

In LLMs, the Circle of Fifths organizes semantic concepts in latent space. Each key represents a different "tonality" or style of language generation, with relationships based on information geometric distance.

Harmonic Prompt Engineering

Instead of using keywords, navigate the circle to modulate output style. Moving to a "sharper" key adds technical jargon, while moving to a "flatter" key simplifies language.

Geometric Chain-of-Thought

Multi-agent systems can use the circle for structured reasoning. Different agents work on adjacent keys (related concepts) to explore a topic from multiple perspectives without repetition.

Tritone Hallucination Detection

The tritone (opposite side of circle) represents maximum semantic tension. Sudden jumps to the opposite key may indicate contradictory or hallucinatory output that needs correction.
