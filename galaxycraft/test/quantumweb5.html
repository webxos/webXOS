Wave-Particle Duality

A core quantum mechanical principle stating that fundamental entities like light and electrons possess both wave-like and particle-like properties

Key Aspects of Wave-Particle Duality

Light as both wave and particle

  • Wave properties: Light exhibits diffraction and interference, classic wave phenomena
  • Particle properties: Light also interacts as discrete packets of energy called photons

Matter as both wave and particle

  • Particle properties: Electrons and other matter particles have mass and can be localized
  • Wave properties: Electrons exhibit wave-like behavior like interference and diffraction

Origin and Development

  • Max Planck (1900): Suggested energy is emitted in discrete units
  • Albert Einstein (1905): Proposed light is quantized into photons
  • Louis de Broglie (1924): Extended duality to matter particles

Significance in Quantum Mechanics

  • A foundational concept of quantum mechanics
  • Behavior depends on experimental observation
  • Challenges classical physics concepts
  • Essential for modern technologies like lasers and semiconductors
